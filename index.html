The Novel & Its History

Overview

  • El Filibusterismo is the second novel by José Rizal and the direct sequel to Noli Me Tangere. Written in Spanish.
  • English title: "The Reign of Greed"
  • Contains 38 chapters.
  • Rizal dedicated the novel to the three martyred priests of the Cavite Mutiny — their execution greatly influenced the novel's message and title.

Dedication — The GOMBURZA

  • Mariano Gómez
  • José Burgos
  • Jacinto Zamora

Their execution profoundly shaped the tone and revolutionary spirit of the novel.

Meaning of "Filibustero"

  • Rizal explained the term to his German friend Ferdinand Blumentritt because it was little known in the Philippines.
  • During the Spanish period, the word was used to label anyone suspected of being a revolutionary or dangerous patriot.
  • Educated Filipinos feared the term because it was associated with rebellion and execution.

Writing & Publication

Timeline

  • Calamba October 1887 — Rizal began writing the novel.
  • London 1888 — Revised parts of the manuscript.
  • Paris / Brussels Continued writing; moved to Brussels to focus on finishing.
  • Biarritz March 29, 1891 — Rizal completed the novel.
  • Ghent, Belgium September 18, 1891 — Novel published with financial help from Valentin Ventura.

Additional Facts

  • Rizal spent 3 years writing the novel.
  • Copies were sent to Ferdinand Blumentritt, Mariano Ponce, Graciano López Jaena, Antonio Luna, and Juan Luna.
  • The original manuscript is preserved at the National Library of the Philippines.

Objectives of Dr. Rizal

01
Defend Filipinos from accusations of ignorance and foolishness.
02
Show the suffering of Filipinos under Spanish colonial rule.
03
Discuss the effects of religion and beliefs in daily life.
04
Expose corruption, cruelty, and abuse in the government.

Synopsis

  • Crisostomo Ibarra returns in disguise as a wealthy jeweler named Simoun, seeking revenge against the Spanish government.
  • Simoun plans a violent revolution and recruits Basilio (now a medical student) to trigger an uprising via a bomb explosion at a social gathering.
  • Isagani — warned by Basilio — realizes his beloved is inside the building and throws the bomb into the river, foiling the plan.
  • After the failed revolution, Simoun takes poison and seeks refuge at the house of Father Florentino, who hears his confession before his death.
  • Father Florentino throws Simoun's jewels into the sea, declaring that treasures used for corruption should someday serve a noble purpose.

Characters

SI
Simoun
Protagonist
Crisostomo Ibarra in disguise as a wealthy jeweler. Seeks revenge against Spain. Symbolizes anger, revenge, and revolution.
BA
Basilio
Educated Youth
Son of Sisa (from Noli). Medical student, initially peaceful, later influenced by Simoun. Represents educated Filipino youth.
IS
Isagani
Idealist
Poet and idealistic student; boyfriend of Paulita Gomez. Throws the bomb into the river to save lives. Represents patriotism and idealism.
MC
Maria Clara
Symbol
Ibarra's former lover; appears briefly. Her suffering deeply affects Simoun. Symbolizes the abused and oppressed Filipina.
JU
Juli
Innocent Victim
Basilio's sweetheart. Sacrifices herself to protect her dignity. Represents innocent Filipinos suffering from abuse and injustice.
PG
Paulita Gomez
Social Climber
Beautiful and wealthy; leaves Isagani for Juanito Pelaez. Represents those attracted to wealth and social status.
JP
Juanito Pelaez
Privileged
Rich student favored by friars; marries Paulita Gomez. Represents privileged Filipinos who benefit from colonial society.
KT
Kabesang Tales
Rebel
Farmer whose land was taken by the friars. Becomes a rebel because of injustice. Represents Filipinos driven to rebellion.
FF
Father Florentino
Moral Voice
Wise and patriotic Filipino priest. Gives moral reflections at the novel's end. Represents hope, wisdom, and true patriotism.
PC
Padre Camorra
Corrupt Friar
Corrupt and abusive friar responsible for Juli's tragic fate. Represents immoral religious officials.
PS
Padre Salvi
Hypocrite
Former parish priest of San Diego; manipulative and cruel. Symbolizes hypocrisy among friars.
DC
Don Custodio
Incompetent Official
Government official who rejects the students' request for a Spanish academy. Represents incompetent officials.
BZ
Ben Zayb
Biased Media
Journalist who twists the truth. Represents biased media and propaganda.
PI
Padre Irene
Selfish Clergy
Adviser to students who pretends to help but serves his own interests. Represents selfish clergy members.
PP
Placido Penitente
Frustrated Student
Student frustrated with the education system. Represents students suffering under unfair education.
TS
Tandang Selo
Helpless Victim
Juli's grandfather; suffers silently from injustice. Represents helpless victims of oppression.
MA
Makaraig
Reformist
Wealthy student who supports the Spanish academy project. Represents educated reformists.
PE
Pecson
Skeptic
Student known for sarcastic comments. Represents skepticism and criticism.
